# Sample Company Handbook

## 1. Welcome

Welcome to **del Digital Solutions**.

This handbook explains our company policies, employee expectations, benefits, and workplace procedures. It is designed to help employees understand how we work and where to find support.

This handbook applies to all employees, contractors, and interns unless a specific policy states otherwise.

---

## 2. Company Overview

**Company Name:** del Digital Solutions  
**Industry:** Software and Data Services  
**Mission:** To build reliable digital solutions that help businesses make better decisions.  
**Values:**

- Integrity
- Ownership
- Collaboration
- Continuous learning
- Customer focus
- Respect for others

---

## 3. Employment Basics

### 3.1 Employment Types

Employees may be classified as:

- **Full-time employees**: Work a standard weekly schedule and are eligible for company benefits.
- **Part-time employees**: Work fewer hours and may have limited benefits.
- **Contractors**: Provide services under a separate agreement.
- **Interns**: Participate in temporary learning or training programs.

### 3.2 Probation Period

New employees may have a probation period of **three months**. During this time, performance, role fit, and expectations are reviewed.

Successful completion of probation does not guarantee permanent employment unless stated in the employment agreement.

---

## 4. Working Hours

### 4.1 Standard Working Hours

The standard workweek is **Monday to Friday**, from **9:00 AM to 6:00 PM**, including a one-hour lunch break.

Teams may agree on different schedules depending on business needs.

### 4.2 Flexible Work

Employees may use flexible working hours with manager approval. Employees are expected to attend required meetings and remain available during agreed core hours.

### 4.3 Remote Work

Remote work is allowed when approved by the employee’s manager.

Remote employees are expected to:

- Maintain reliable internet access
- Be available during working hours
- Protect company data
- Join meetings on time
- Keep their calendar updated

---

## 5. Attendance and Absences

Employees should inform their manager as soon as possible if they are late, unavailable, or unable to work.

Unplanned absences should be reported before the start of the working day whenever possible.

Repeated unexplained absences may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 6. Leave Policy

### 6.1 Annual Leave

Full-time employees are entitled to **20 working days of paid annual leave per calendar year**.

Leave requests should be submitted at least **two weeks in advance**, unless the absence is urgent.

Managers approve leave based on team availability and business needs.

### 6.2 Sick Leave

Employees who are sick should notify their manager as early as possible.

For sick leave longer than **three consecutive working days**, the company may request medical documentation, depending on local law.

### 6.3 Public Holidays

The company observes official public holidays in the employee’s country of employment.

### 6.4 Unpaid Leave

Unpaid leave may be granted at the company’s discretion. Employees should discuss unpaid leave requests with their manager and HR.

### 6.5 Parental Leave

Parental leave is provided according to local employment law and company policy. Employees should contact HR for details.

---

## 7. Compensation and Payroll

### 7.1 Salary

Employees are paid according to their employment agreement. Salary is confidential and should not be shared unless required by law or company policy.

### 7.2 Payroll Schedule

Salary is paid monthly, usually on the last business day of the month.

### 7.3 Bonuses

Bonuses may be awarded based on company performance, individual performance, or specific agreements. Bonuses are discretionary unless guaranteed in writing.

### 7.4 Overtime

Overtime must be approved by a manager in advance. Overtime compensation depends on local law and the employee’s contract.

---

## 8. Benefits

Eligible employees may receive the following benefits:

- Health insurance
- Paid annual leave
- Sick leave
- Learning and development budget
- Remote work equipment support
- Wellness programs
- Referral bonuses

Benefit availability may depend on employment type, location, and length of service.

---

## 9. Performance Management

### 9.1 Performance Reviews

Performance reviews are usually conducted twice per year.

Reviews may include:

- Goal progress
- Quality of work
- Communication
- Teamwork
- Ownership
- Growth areas

### 9.2 Goals

Employees and managers should agree on clear goals. Goals should be measurable, realistic, and aligned with company priorities.

### 9.3 Promotions

Promotions are based on performance, skills, business needs, and demonstrated readiness for the next level.

---

## 10. Code of Conduct

Employees are expected to behave professionally and respectfully.

Employees must:

- Treat others with respect
- Communicate honestly
- Follow company policies
- Protect confidential information
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Use company resources responsibly

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, bullying, fraud, theft, violence, and misuse of company systems.

---

## 11. Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination

The company does not tolerate harassment or discrimination based on personal characteristics such as age, race, gender, religion, disability, nationality, sexual orientation, or any other protected status.

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it to HR, their manager, or another trusted leader.

Reports will be handled confidentially where possible.

---

## 12. Diversity and Inclusion

The company is committed to building an inclusive workplace where people feel respected and valued.

Employees are expected to support a culture of openness, fairness, and equal opportunity.

---

## 13. Confidentiality

Employees may have access to confidential company, customer, employee, or partner information.

Confidential information must not be shared outside the company without authorization.

Examples of confidential information include:

- Customer data
- Financial information
- Business plans
- Product roadmaps
- Source code
- Employee records
- Vendor contracts

Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

---

## 14. Information Security

Employees must follow security practices to protect company data.

Employees should:

- Use strong passwords
- Enable multi-factor authentication
- Lock devices when away
- Avoid sharing login credentials
- Report suspicious emails or security incidents
- Store files only in approved systems

Company data should not be stored on personal devices unless approved.

---

## 15. Use of Company Equipment

Company equipment is provided for work purposes.

Employees are responsible for keeping equipment safe and in good condition.

Lost, stolen, or damaged equipment should be reported immediately.

Upon leaving the company, employees must return all company property.

---

## 16. Internet, Email, and Software Use

Company systems should be used responsibly and primarily for work purposes.

Employees must not:

- Install unauthorized software
- Access illegal or harmful content
- Share confidential data through personal accounts
- Use company tools for personal commercial activity
- Bypass security controls

---

## 17. Conflict of Interest

Employees should avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests.

Examples include:

- Working for a competitor
- Accepting gifts that influence decisions
- Hiring relatives without disclosure
- Using company information for personal gain

Potential conflicts should be reported to HR or management.

---

## 18. Gifts and Hospitality

Employees may accept modest gifts or hospitality if they are reasonable and do not influence business decisions.

Expensive gifts, cash, or inappropriate benefits must be declined and reported.

---

## 19. Travel and Expenses

Business travel must be approved in advance.

Employees should keep receipts and submit expense reports within **30 days**.

Reimbursable expenses may include:

- Transportation
- Accommodation
- Meals during business travel
- Approved business tools or services

Personal expenses are not reimbursable.

---

## 20. Training and Development

The company supports employee growth through training, mentoring, and learning resources.

Employees may request support for:

- Courses
- Certifications
- Conferences
- Books
- Professional memberships

Approval depends on budget, role relevance, and business priorities.

---

## 21. Health and Safety

Employees are expected to maintain a safe working environment.

Employees should report hazards, injuries, or unsafe conditions to HR or management.

Remote employees should also maintain a safe and ergonomic workspace.

---

## 22. Disciplinary Policy

The company may take disciplinary action when employees violate policies or fail to meet expectations.

Disciplinary steps may include:

1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Performance improvement plan
4. Suspension
5. Termination

The company may skip steps depending on the seriousness of the issue.

---

## 23. Grievance Procedure

Employees who have concerns should first try to resolve them with their manager, if appropriate.

If the issue cannot be resolved, employees may contact HR.

The company will review complaints fairly and take appropriate action.

---

## 24. Resignation and Termination

### 24.1 Resignation

Employees should provide written notice according to their employment agreement.

A common notice period is **two weeks** or **one month**, depending on role and location.

### 24.2 Termination

Employment may end due to resignation, mutual agreement, redundancy, misconduct, poor performance, or other lawful reasons.

### 24.3 Exit Process

Before leaving, employees must:

- Return company equipment
- Transfer work documents
- Complete handover tasks
- Remove company data from personal devices
- Attend an exit interview if requested

---

## 25. HR Contact

Employees may contact HR for questions about:

- Leave
- Payroll
- Benefits
- Contracts
- Workplace concerns
- Policy clarification
- Employee records

**HR Email:** hr@deldigital.example  
**HR Working Hours:** Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM
