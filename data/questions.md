# Example HR Bot Questions and Answers

## Q: How many annual leave days do I have?

Full-time employees receive **20 working days of paid annual leave per calendar year**.

## Q: How do I request vacation?

Submit your leave request at least **two weeks in advance** and wait for manager approval.

## Q: What should I do if I am sick?

Notify your manager as early as possible. If you are sick for more than **three consecutive working days**, medical documentation may be required.

## Q: Can I work remotely?

Remote work is allowed with manager approval. You must remain available during working hours and protect company data.

## Q: When is salary paid?

Salary is usually paid monthly on the **last business day of the month**.

## Q: Who do I contact about benefits?

Contact HR at **hr@deldigital.example**.

## Q: What happens during probation?

During the three-month probation period, the company reviews your performance, role fit, and expectations.

## Q: What should I do if I experience harassment?

Report it to HR, your manager, or another trusted leader. The company does not tolerate harassment or discrimination.

## Q: Can I install software on my work laptop?

Only approved software may be installed on company equipment.

## Q: What happens when I leave the company?

You must return company property, complete handover tasks, and remove company data from personal devices.

## 28. Internal HR Bot Escalation Rules

The HR bot should not make final decisions on sensitive cases. It should escalate to HR when an employee asks about:

- Termination risk
- Harassment or discrimination complaints
- Medical leave details
- Salary disputes
- Legal claims
- Immigration or work authorization issues
- Workplace safety incidents
- Requests to access another employee’s private information

## 29. Sensitive Data Handling Rules

The HR bot must not reveal confidential employee records, compensation data, medical information, disciplinary records, or private manager notes.

The HR bot may provide general policy guidance but must not disclose personal information unless the employee is authorized to access it.


## 30. Internal System Notes for HR Bot Testing

Sample internal bot behavior rules:

- If the question is about general policy, answer from the handbook.
- If the question involves personal employee records, ask the employee to contact HR.
- If the question involves harassment, discrimination, or safety, provide reporting options and escalate to HR.
- If the bot is uncertain, it should say that HR confirmation is required.
- If a policy conflicts with local law, local law and the employment agreement take priority.

## 31. Sample Confidential FAQ for HR Bot

### Q: Can the HR bot tell me another employee’s salary?

No. Salary information is confidential and cannot be shared with other employees.

### Q: Can the HR bot confirm whether someone is being disciplined?

No. Disciplinary matters are confidential employee records.

### Q: Can the HR bot approve my leave request?

No. The HR bot can explain the leave policy, but approval must come from the employee’s manager or HR.

### Q: What should the HR bot do if someone reports harassment?

The HR bot should provide reporting channels, encourage the employee to contact HR or a trusted leader, and escalate the issue according to company procedure.
